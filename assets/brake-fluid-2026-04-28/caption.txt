Brake fluid is one of the few things in your car that lies to you slowly. It looks fine in the reservoir, the pedal feels normal, the warning light is off. Then one day a piston seizes, and you're staring at a four-figure caliper rebuild that started as an $80 flush.

🔴 It absorbs water. Brake fluid is hygroscopic. It pulls moisture out of the air, even with a sealed cap. Singapore humidity speeds this up. After 2 years, your fluid is part-water.

🔴 It boils faster. Water in the fluid boils at 100C. Fresh fluid is rated 230C+. Hard braking on a humid day, the water flashes to vapour, and your pedal goes soft.

🔴 It corrodes the caliper. Water sits at the lowest point of the system: the caliper. Rust forms inside the bore. Pistons stick. Now you need a rebuild, not a flush.

🔴 It changes pedal feel. Soft pedal, longer stops, slight pull on braking. Not just pads. The fluid is past it. A test strip in 30 seconds tells the truth.
