Skip an oil change long enough and the oil thickens into sludge. Sludge plugs the narrow oil galleries that feed the cam and bearings. Once those block, the parts that need oil don't get any. That's how an oil change becomes an engine rebuild.

🔴 Sludge blocks oil galleries. Old oil thickens until it can't move through the engine. Plugs the passages that feed the moving parts.

🔴 Variable valve timing fails. VVT solenoids need clean oil to actuate. Sludge stops them. Check engine light. Performance drops. Replacement is hundreds, not thousands. Catch it early.

🔴 Cam follower wears out. Without oil flow, the cam lobe wears flat against the follower. Valve no longer opens fully. Engine runs rough. The bill starts climbing.

🔴 Engine rebuild. Once bearings score and pistons gall, you're in 4-figure territory. We've seen $5,000+ jobs that started as a skipped $120 oil change. Same car, same owner.
