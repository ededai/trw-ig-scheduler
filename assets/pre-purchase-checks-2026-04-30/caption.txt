Buying second-hand. Four checks before you sign.

Most second-hand cars in SG are fine. The ones that aren't can cost you four figures in the first six months. These four checks take ten minutes and tell you 80 percent of what you need to know.

🔴 Match the chassis numbers. VIN on the dashboard, on the door pillar, and on LTA. All three must match. A mismatch means accident damage, a panel swap, or a salvaged write-off. Walk away.

🔴 Pull the dipsticks. Engine oil should be amber, not black. Coolant should be pink or green, not rust-brown. Brake fluid should be clear, not opaque. Cloudy fluids mean neglected service.

🔴 Run a diagnostic scan. A $20 OBD2 scanner reads stored fault codes that the dashboard never showed you. Some sellers clear them right before viewing. Stored codes always come back if the issue is real.

🔴 Drive it cold and warm. Start it cold. Listen for cold-start rattle. Drive it 20 minutes. Repeat the test cold the next day. Some faults only show on the first start of the morning.
