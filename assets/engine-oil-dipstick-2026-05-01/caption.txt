Your dipstick tells you four things in 30 seconds. Three are visual. One you smell.

Most drivers pull the dipstick once a year, see oil on it, slide it back in. The dipstick is one of the most useful diagnostic tools your car has, and it costs nothing to use.

🔴 The level. Pull, wipe, dip, read. Oil sits between MIN and MAX. Below MIN means you're burning oil or leaking. Above MAX means coolant or fuel contamination. Either way, get it checked.

🔴 The colour. Fresh oil is light amber. After 5,000 km it darkens. By 10,000 km it should be dark amber, not jet black. Black, opaque, no flow off the stick: past its service life.

🔴 The smell. Healthy oil smells like oil. If you smell petrol, you have fuel dilution. Common on turbo cars doing short SG trips. Get an oil change earlier than the manual says.

🔴 The texture. Rub a drop between two fingers. Smooth slip = healthy. Gritty = metal particles, internal wear. Milky pink = coolant in the oil. Both mean stop driving and call us.
