Singapore heat is brutal on cooling systems. Your car warns you four different ways.

Coolant lives a hard life in SG. Constant ambient heat, stop-start traffic, the AC running every drive. The cooling system has more to do here than in most countries. The dashboard tells you when it's losing the fight.

🔴 Coolant level (yellow). The reservoir is below MIN. Could be evaporation, could be a slow leak. Top up with the right grade and watch it. If it drops again in days, find the leak.

🔴 Temperature (red). Needle in the red, or a thermometer icon glowing. Pull over, switch off, let it cool 30 minutes. Driving on damages the cylinder head. Tow it instead of risking the rebuild.

🔴 Loss of pressure (yellow). Common on European cars with electronic cap sensors. Means the cap, hose, or reservoir is leaking pressure. The system can't reach proper boiling point. Cheap fix if caught early.

🔴 Service due (yellow). Some cars track coolant age and prompt a flush at 4 to 5 years. SG humidity ages coolant faster than the brochure assumes. Don't ignore even if temps look normal.
