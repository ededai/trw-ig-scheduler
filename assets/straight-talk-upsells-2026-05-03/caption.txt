4 service add-ons that fatten your bill but rarely move the needle on your car.

Some workshops push the same upsells at every visit. Some you genuinely need now and then. None of these belong on every receipt. Here's where to push back.

🔴 Air filter at every service. Most filters last 20,000 to 40,000 km. If your workshop charges to swap it every 5,000 km, that's pure margin. Look at it, judge by colour, change only when dirty.

🔴 Engine flush before oil change. Modern engine oils already contain detergents. A pre-change flush rarely helps a maintained engine and can dislodge sludge into worse places. Skip unless oil is heavily contaminated.

🔴 Premium oil grade for normal driving. Your manual specifies a grade. SG climate doesn't change that. Paying for top-tier synthetic when the car was designed for mid-tier is money the engine doesn't see.

🔴 Brake fluid flush every service. Brake fluid genuinely needs flushing every 2 years. Workshops that push it at every interval are inflating the bill. Test the fluid first. Flush only if needed.
