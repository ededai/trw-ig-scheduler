Three lights. One battery. One of them means stop driving now.

Most drivers see the battery icon and assume it's just the battery. It's almost never just the battery. The icon doubles as a charging-system warning, an alternator warning, and a belt warning. Telling them apart matters.

🔴 Battery icon, solid red. Your alternator has stopped charging. The car is running off battery alone. You have 15 to 30 minutes of driving before everything dies. Drive to a workshop now, not later.

🔴 Battery icon, flickering. Loose terminal, weak alternator brushes, or a worn drive belt. Not immediate failure, but the alternator is on the way out. Test it in days, not weeks.

🔴 Check engine + battery together. Both lights on usually mean a charging-system fault the ECU has logged. The engine still runs, but the diagnosis matters. Don't ignore it before a road trip.

🔴 Slow crank, no light. The engine cranks slowly on cold starts, no warning at all. That's the battery itself, not the charging system. 4 to 5 year old batteries die without warning in SG heat.
